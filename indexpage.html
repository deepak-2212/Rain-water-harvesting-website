About
Water Harvesting


Water harvesting is all about collecting and storing rainwater for later use, instead of letting it run off unused. It's a way to conserve this precious resource, especially in areas facing dry periods or droughts.
Imagine collecting free water from the sky! This saved water can be used for watering gardens, flushing toilets, or even drinking with proper treatment. It's a lifesaver in drought-prone areas and reduces the burden on our freshwater supplies. Techniques range from simple rooftop collection systems with gutters and tanks to channeling runoff from driveways. By embracing water harvesting, we can become smarter water users and ensure a more sustainable future.

Prototyping And Iteration


Developing effective water harvesting techniques relies heavily on prototyping and iteration. This allows for continuous testing, improvement, and ultimately, cost-effective solutions. we build a small, cheap model (prototype) of our water harvesting idea first! then we test it with fake rain, then we observe how well it captures water, and fix any flaws. By repeating this build-test-improve loop, we can develop a system that works perfectly for our needs without wasting money on a big mistake.

Process

Collection: Rainwater is captured from surfaces like rooftops, or directed from land areas using channels.
Storage: The collected water is stored in tanks, cisterns, or even redirected to replenish groundwater sources.
Treatment: Depending on the intended use, the water might need filtration or other treatment methods.
Reuse: The stored water can be used for various purposes, like irrigation, flushing toilets, or even drinking (with proper treatment).
